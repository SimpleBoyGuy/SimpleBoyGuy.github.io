Research Project

RL Solver for FJSP

Reinforcement Learning for the Flexible Job Shop Scheduling Problem

A reinforcement learning solver for flexible job shop scheduling, designed to balance solution quality, decision transparency, and computational efficiency.

Problem at a Glance

In the Flexible Job Shop Scheduling Problem, each job contains an ordered sequence of operations, and each operation may be processed by multiple eligible machines. The solver must decide both operation sequencing and machine assignment, with the objective of minimizing the makespan.

Solver Design

The current solver uses a problem-specific RL formulation together with an action-priority policy design. In the current paper, APDPO is one key policy optimization component inside this broader RL solver pipeline.

01

State Encoding

Represent job progress, machine availability, active work, and system time as the decision state.

02

Action Priorities

Assign each feasible machine-operation action a priority value to define the scheduling policy.

03

Trajectory Rollout

Generate scheduling trajectories, evaluate returns, and compare decisions using makespan-driven rewards.

04

Direct Update

Promote effective actions and suppress competing ones through a direct gradient-free update rule.

Quality

Move RL solutions closer to strong optimization baselines on standard FJSP benchmarks.

Transparency

Use action priorities to make policy behavior easier to interpret than purely opaque decision rules.

Efficiency

Reduce update complexity through direct policy modification rather than relying only on gradient-based learning.

Results

On representative benchmark groups from the Brandimarte and Hurink families, the reported results show that the solver outperforms existing RL baselines and moves closer to OR-Tools.
